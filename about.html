About Us

Our Mission

Peoples Oakland strives to be a premier recovery and wellness center, and is committed to holistic, comprehensive, member driven recovery. To carry out holistic and innovative approaches to mental health, we have constructed a facility with a multi-purpose social center, a commercial kitchen, a fitness center equipped with fitness machines and with space for yoga, dance and exercise classes, meeting rooms, offices, a large secure outside deck and a resource and information center staffed by peers and equipped with computers for access to e-mail and to the internet.

Members

Peoples Oakland currently serves over 200 adults throughout Allegheny County in treatment for chronic severe mental illness (SMI). Today we offer a broad range of events and services to our members, including social and recreational services, individual and group counseling, and employment assistance.

Community-based Goals

Peoples Oakland is committed to continuing to build productive partnerships and networks with other service providers, community partners, institutions, foundations, the Allegheny County Department of Human Services, the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, the City of Pittsburgh, and the Federal Government. Peoples Oakland is part of a neighborhood partnership program, working with multiple community-based partners, institutions, and public bodies to improve Oakland as a place to live, work, learn, and have access to medical care and provide regional cultural amenities.

Outcomes

Peoples Oakland has made a major positive impact on its members. Our programs including daily counseling and social rehabilitation have helped members gain control of their lifes and use the services to grow, change, and recover. In addition, Peoples Oakland counselors offer crisis intervention services to resolve member crises across a variety of domains.

In the year 2020-2021:

  • 74 out of 87 crises/potential crises were resolved.
  • 96.6% of all documented crises/potential crises were deescalated after the immediate intervention.
  • 90% of members loved the services they received.
  • Members who have attended Peoples Oakland for at least 6 months showed a significant increase in levels of social connectedness.
